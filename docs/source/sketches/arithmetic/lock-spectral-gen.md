## Arithmetic Formulation

### Setup

"Spectral generator" in arithmetic means:
- Fundamental objects (newforms, primitive characters) generate spectrum
- L-functions are generated by primitive L-functions
- Hecke algebra is generated by finitely many operators

### Statement (Arithmetic Version)

**Theorem (Arithmetic Spectral Generation).** Spectra are finitely generated:

1. **Newform generation:** All modular forms are generated by newforms
2. **Primitive generation:** All L-functions factor through primitive L-functions
3. **Hecke generation:** $\mathcal{H}$ is generated by $T_p$ for primes $p$

---

### Proof

**Step 1: Newform Generation**

**Decomposition [Atkin-Lehner 1970]:**
$$S_k(\Gamma_0(N)) = \bigoplus_{M | N} \bigoplus_{f \in S_k^{\text{new}}(\Gamma_0(M))} V_f$$

where $V_f$ is spanned by $f(z), f(dz)$ for $d | N/M$.

**Generation:** Every modular form is a linear combination of newforms and their "oldform" translates.

**Proof:** The newform decomposition is orthogonal for Petersson inner product.

**Step 2: Primitive L-function Generation**

**Imprimitive L-function:**
$$L(s, \chi \chi_0) = L(s, \chi) \cdot \prod_{p | q/q'} (1 - \chi(p)p^{-s})$$

where $\chi$ has conductor $q'$ and $\chi_0$ is principal mod $q$.

**Generation:** Every Dirichlet L-function factors:
$$L(s, \chi) = L(s, \chi^*) \cdot \text{(Euler factors)}$$

where $\chi^*$ is the primitive character inducing $\chi$.

**Step 3: Hecke Algebra Generation**

**Hecke algebra:** $\mathcal{H} = \mathbb{Z}[T_n : n \geq 1]$

**Generation:** $\mathcal{H}$ is generated by $\{T_p : p \text{ prime}\}$.

**Proof:** The multiplicativity relations:
$$T_{mn} = T_m T_n \quad \text{for } (m, n) = 1$$
$$T_{p^{k+1}} = T_p T_{p^k} - p^{k-1} T_{p^{k-1}} \quad \text{for } p | N$$

reduce all $T_n$ to products of $T_p$.

**Step 4: Galois Generation**

**Galois group:** $G = \text{Gal}(K/\mathbb{Q})$

**Generation:** $G$ is generated by Frobenius elements:
$$G = \langle \text{Frob}_p : p \text{ unramified} \rangle$$

**Proof [Čebotarev]:** Frobenius elements are dense in $G$ (in profinite topology).

**Step 5: Motivic Generation**

**Category of motives:** $\mathcal{M}_K$

**Generators:**
- Tate motive $\mathbb{Q}(1)$
- Motives of curves, abelian varieties
- Artin motives

**Generation [Grothendieck standard conjectures]:**
Every motive is a summand of a product of curve motives.

**Step 6: Spectral Generation Certificate**

The spectral generation certificate:
$$K_{\text{Gen}}^+ = (\text{spectral space}, \text{generators}, \text{generation proof})$$

**Components:**
- **Space:** Modular forms, L-functions, Hecke algebra
- **Generators:** Newforms, primitive characters, $T_p$
- **Proof:** How every element decomposes
