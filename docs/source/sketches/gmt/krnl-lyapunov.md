# KRNL-Lyapunov: Canonical Lyapunov Functional — GMT Translation

## Original Statement (Hypostructure)

Given a hypostructure with dissipation, compactness, and stiffness permits, there exists a canonical Lyapunov functional characterized as the optimal transport cost to the equilibrium manifold.

## GMT Setting

**Ambient Space:** $(X, d)$ — complete geodesic metric space (length space)

**Measure Structure:** $\mu \in \mathcal{M}^+(X)$ — reference Radon measure

**Energy Functional:** $\Phi: X \to [0, \infty]$ — proper lower semicontinuous

**Metric Slope:** $|\partial \Phi|(x) := \limsup_{y \to x} \frac{[\Phi(x) - \Phi(y)]^+}{d(x,y)}$

**Safe Manifold:** $M := \{x \in X : |\partial \Phi|(x) = 0\}$ — critical set (equilibria)

**Gradient Flow:** $S_t: X \to X$ — semigroup generated by the metric gradient of $\Phi$

## GMT Statement

**Theorem (Optimal Transport Lyapunov Functional).** Let $(X, d)$ be a complete geodesic metric space and $\Phi: X \to [0, \infty]$ satisfy:

1. **(Dissipation)** The gradient flow $(S_t)$ satisfies the Energy-Dissipation Inequality:
   $$\Phi(S_t x) + \int_0^t |\partial \Phi|^2(S_s x) \, ds \leq \Phi(x)$$

2. **(Compactness)** Sublevel sets $\{x : \Phi(x) \leq c\}$ are compact in $(X, d)$

3. **(Stiffness)** The Łojasiewicz-Simon inequality holds near $M$:
   $$|\partial \Phi|(x) \geq C \cdot |\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min}|^{1-\theta}$$
   for some $\theta \in (0, 1)$ and $C > 0$

Then the **canonical Lyapunov functional** is:
$$\mathcal{L}(x) := \inf_{y \in M} \left\{ \Phi(y) + \mathcal{C}(x, y) \right\}$$

where $\mathcal{C}(x, y)$ is the **geodesic action cost**:
$$\mathcal{C}(x, y) := \inf_{\gamma: x \to y} \int_0^1 |\partial \Phi|(\gamma(s)) \cdot |\dot{\gamma}|(s) \, ds$$

This functional satisfies:

**(A)** $\mathcal{L}(S_t x) \leq \mathcal{L}(x)$ with equality iff $x \in M$

**(B)** $\mathcal{L}(x) = \Phi_{\min}$ iff $x \in M$

**(C)** $\mathcal{L}(x) - \Phi_{\min} \asymp (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})$ on compact sublevel sets

## Proof Sketch

### Step 1: Action Cost and Jacobi Metric

Define the **Jacobi metric** (conformal to $d$):
$$d_\Phi(x, y) := \inf_{\gamma: x \to y} \int_0^1 \sqrt{|\partial \Phi|(\gamma(s))} \cdot |\dot{\gamma}|(s) \, ds$$

**Relation to Action Cost:** By Cauchy-Schwarz:
$$\mathcal{C}(x, y) \geq d_\Phi(x, y)^2 / L(\gamma)$$

with equality achieved by the gradient flow curve (constant-speed parametrization of the steepest descent).

**Geodesic Structure:** $(X, d_\Phi)$ is a length space. The action cost is the **squared Jacobi distance**:
$$\mathcal{C}(x, y) = d_\Phi(x, y)^2$$

when the infimum is achieved by the gradient flow trajectory.

### Step 2: Well-Definedness of $\mathcal{L}$

**Lower Semicontinuity:** The functional $\mathcal{L}$ is l.s.c. as the infimum of l.s.c. functions.

**Properness:** Since $M \neq \emptyset$ (by compactness of sublevel sets) and $\Phi$ is bounded below, we have $\mathcal{L}(x) < \infty$ for all $x$ with $\Phi(x) < \infty$.

**Attainment:** The infimum in $\mathcal{L}(x)$ is attained by some $y^* \in M$ by:
1. Compactness: $M$ is compact (closed subset of compact sublevel set)
2. Continuity: $y \mapsto \Phi(y) + \mathcal{C}(x, y)$ is l.s.c.
3. Direct method: minimizing sequence has convergent subsequence

### Step 3: Monotonicity Along Gradient Flow (Part A)

**Claim:** $t \mapsto \mathcal{L}(S_t x)$ is non-increasing.

*Proof:* Let $y^* \in M$ achieve the infimum for $\mathcal{L}(x)$. For the gradient flow $S_t x$:
$$\mathcal{L}(S_t x) \leq \Phi(y^*) + \mathcal{C}(S_t x, y^*)$$

The action cost from $S_t x$ to $y^*$ satisfies:
$$\mathcal{C}(S_t x, y^*) \leq \mathcal{C}(S_t x, x) + \mathcal{C}(x, y^*)$$

But the gradient flow itself provides a path from $x$ to $S_t x$ with:
$$\mathcal{C}(x, S_t x) = \int_0^t |\partial \Phi|^2(S_s x) \, ds$$

By the Energy-Dissipation Inequality:
$$\Phi(S_t x) - \Phi(x) \leq -\int_0^t |\partial \Phi|^2(S_s x) \, ds = -\mathcal{C}(x, S_t x)$$

Combining:
$$\mathcal{L}(S_t x) \leq \Phi(y^*) + \mathcal{C}(x, y^*) - \mathcal{C}(x, S_t x) = \mathcal{L}(x) - \mathcal{C}(x, S_t x) \leq \mathcal{L}(x)$$

**Strict Decrease:** If $x \notin M$, then $|\partial \Phi|(x) > 0$, so $\mathcal{C}(x, S_t x) > 0$ for $t > 0$.

### Step 4: Characterization of Minimum (Part B)

**Claim:** $\mathcal{L}(x) = \Phi_{\min}$ iff $x \in M$.

*Proof of $\Leftarrow$:* If $x \in M$, then:
$$\mathcal{L}(x) = \inf_{y \in M} \{\Phi(y) + \mathcal{C}(x, y)\} \leq \Phi(x) + \mathcal{C}(x, x) = \Phi(x) + 0$$

Since $x \in M$ implies $\Phi(x) = \Phi_{\min}$ (by stiffness — critical points are minima), we get $\mathcal{L}(x) \leq \Phi_{\min}$.

But also $\mathcal{L}(x) \geq \Phi(y^*) \geq \Phi_{\min}$ for any $y^* \in M$.

*Proof of $\Rightarrow$:* If $\mathcal{L}(x) = \Phi_{\min}$, then the infimum $\Phi(y^*) + \mathcal{C}(x, y^*) = \Phi_{\min}$ implies:
- $\Phi(y^*) = \Phi_{\min}$ (since $\Phi \geq \Phi_{\min}$)
- $\mathcal{C}(x, y^*) = 0$

The second condition implies $d_\Phi(x, y^*) = 0$. By the Łojasiewicz inequality, $d_\Phi$ is a genuine metric near $M$. Hence $x = y^* \in M$.

### Step 5: Height Equivalence (Part C)

**Upper Bound:** For $x$ with $\Phi(x) \leq c$, let $y^* = \arg\min_{y \in M} d(x, y)$. Then:
$$\mathcal{L}(x) - \Phi_{\min} \leq \mathcal{C}(x, y^*) \leq C_1 \cdot d(x, M)^2 \leq C_2 \cdot (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})$$

The last inequality uses the **coercivity** of $\Phi$ near $M$: there exists $c_0 > 0$ such that:
$$\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min} \geq c_0 \cdot d(x, M)^2$$

**Lower Bound:** By the Łojasiewicz inequality:
$$|\partial \Phi|(x) \geq C \cdot (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})^{1-\theta}$$

The gradient flow from $x$ to $M$ has length:
$$\int_0^\infty |\dot{S}_t x| \, dt = \int_0^\infty |\partial \Phi|(S_t x) \, dt \geq \text{const} \cdot (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})^{\theta}$$

Hence $\mathcal{C}(x, y^*) \geq c_3 \cdot (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})^{2\theta}$.

**Combining:** For $\theta \in [\frac{1}{2}, 1)$:
$$c_3 (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})^{2\theta} \leq \mathcal{L}(x) - \Phi_{\min} \leq C_2 (\Phi(x) - \Phi_{\min})$$

This gives the asymptotic equivalence $\mathcal{L} - \Phi_{\min} \asymp (\Phi - \Phi_{\min})$.

## Key GMT Inequalities Used

1. **Energy-Dissipation Identity (AGS):**
   $$\Phi(S_t x) + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t |\dot{\gamma}|^2 + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t |\partial \Phi|^2 \circ \gamma = \Phi(x)$$

2. **Jacobi Metric Definition:**
   $$d_\Phi(x,y) = \inf_\gamma \int_0^1 \sqrt{|\partial \Phi|(\gamma)} \cdot |\dot{\gamma}| \, ds$$

3. **Łojasiewicz-Simon Inequality (Metric Form):**
   $$|\partial \Phi|(x) \geq C |\Phi(x) - \Phi(x^*)|^{1-\theta}$$

4. **Optimal Transport Duality (Kantorovich):**
   $$\mathcal{C}(x,y) = \sup_\phi \left\{ \phi(y) - \phi(x) : |\partial \phi| \leq |\partial \Phi| \right\}$$
